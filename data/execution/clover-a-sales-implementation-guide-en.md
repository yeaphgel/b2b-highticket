## Quick Start (5 Minutes to Understand Core)

### What is Clover A-sales?

Clover A-sales = Time-based rhythm (1-7-3) + 8-Gate closing map (G1-G7) + AI review system

**One sentence**: Anchor the customer's #1 urgent problem in Day 1, build trust over 7 days, then execute 3 promotional rounds over 30 days—while using AI analysis of every call to accelerate your personal growth.

### Why "1-7-3"?

- **1**: In the first 5 minutes of the call, identify the customer's **ONE most urgent problem** (everything else is secondary)
- **7**: Over the next 7 days, use specific content (diagnostic report, case studies, industry insights) to build trust; by Day 7, make a "light-touch close"
- **3**: If not closed by Day 7, invest the next 30 days in **3 promotional rounds** (Day 14, Day 21, Day 30) to close by Day 30

### What are the 8 Gates?

| Gate | Duration | Objective | Completion Criteria |
|------|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **PRE** | - | Screen for right customers | ICP 2-of-3 conditions met |
| **G1** | 3 min | Establish rapport | Customer is relaxed, talking freely |
| **G2** | 5 min | Diagnose pain points | Customer states 2+ real pain points |
| **G3** | 6 min | Amplify problem | Customer shows clear emotional shift |
| **G4** | 5 min | Get commitment | Customer says "I want to fix this now" |
| **G5** | 4 min | Display value | Customer asks "How do you do it?" |
| **G6** | 4 min | Close | Deposit received or contract signed |
| **G7** | 30 days | Nurture | Day 1-30 follow-up rhythm |

**Key rule**: Skip a gate and you lose. Follow the sequence and you win. No progression without completion.

## Implementation Process

### Phase 1: Pre-Call Screening (PRE)

#### 1.1 Confirm ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

**2-of-3 Conditions** (meet 2+ to engage):
- ✓ A: Existing social media presence (Xiaohongshu/TikTok/WeChat) with clear growth needs
- ✓ B: Company has content operations budget (or hiring for this role)
- ✓ C: Decision-maker (CEO/owner) participates, can green-light purchase

**High-risk signals** (be cautious with your time):
- ❌ Only employees attend, decision-maker never shows
- ❌ "Let employees try first" (signals no real decision authority)
- ❌ First question is "Can you discount?" (signals budget constraints)
- ❌ Strong AI resistance without specific objections (signals closed mindset)

#### 1.2 Three Pre-Call Essentials

Complete 24 hours before the call:

```
☐ Scout the competition:
  Review their social media accounts (follower count, posting frequency, content quality)
  Note: When was the last post? What's their growth trajectory?
  
☐ Identify the anxiety point:
  Research their industry's recent competitive moves
  Find 1 "if they don't adapt, they'll fall behind" trigger point
  
☐ Prepare case study:
  Find 1 success story from the same industry (similar scale/audience)
  Memorize 3-5 key metrics (before vs. after)
  Keep in your "back pocket" for G5
```

**In OpenClaw**: Record these three items in the customer's CRM profile, mark their ICP score, flag any red signals.

### Phase 2: Call Execution (G1-G6)

#### 2.1 G1 - Ice Breaker (3 minutes)

**Objective**: Customer feels at ease, talks freely, feels like chatting with a friend (not receiving a pitch)

**Talk Track**:
```
"[Customer name], thanks for taking the time today. I took a look at your account—
[specific data: follower count, posting cadence, content quality].
You've got a solid foundation in content. You're just missing a consistent system.

Today I want to understand your situation better—see if we can help.
If we can't, I'll tell you straight up."

→ Shows you came prepared (authority) + lowers defense walls (consultant mindset)
```

**Things NOT to do**:
- ❌ Launch into product features or system architecture
- ❌ Say "Our AI is extremely advanced"
- ❌ Ask about budget upfront

**Completion criteria**: Customer is relaxed, initiating conversation

**Hermes optimization**: Pre-scan social media, generate a "professional Pre-Call observation," suggest the most credible opening data point

---

#### 2.2 G2 - Diagnose Current State (5 minutes)

**Objective**: Customer describes 2+ real pain points in their own words

**Golden Question Library** (ask in order, adapt based on answers):

```
Q1: Who handles content for you right now?
→ Understand current people/structure

Q2: On a scale of 0-10, how satisfied are you with your account's growth rate?
→ Gauge satisfaction (8-10 = not an ideal prospect)

Q3: Have you tried managing content in-house? Why did you stop?
→ Uncover real pain point ("stopped" is the key signal)

Q4: Is there a competitor's account that makes you think "How are they doing that?"
→ Trigger competitive anxiety

Q5: If content operations stayed exactly as they are now, 
    what would it do to your customer acquisition targets this year?
→ Quantify the impact
```

**Key technique**: After asking, stay quiet. Let them talk.

**Completion criteria**: Customer articulates 2+ pain points in their own language

**Hermes optimization**: Real-time transcription + pain-point detection. If customer's answer is shallow, Hermes suggests next probe question. Auto-generate diagnostic summary at end of G2.

---

#### 2.3 G3 - Extreme Tension Pulling (6 minutes)

**Objective**: Amplify anxiety AND desire, create felt distance between As-Is and To-Be

**Conversion Formula**: Deal = Desire × Trust ÷ Resistance

**Pulling Down** (amplify pain):
```
"You said an employee handles content. But what else do they do?
 If they quit tomorrow, who takes over content?
 Do you have a system to absorb that risk?"

"Your competitor's account went from zero to 150K followers in a year.
 That gap will be bigger next year, won't it?
 When they hit 500K, will you even have a chance to catch up?"

"If content stays unstable all year, what happens to your customer acquisition target?
 How much does that impact your annual revenue?"
```

**Pulling Up** (amplify desire):
```
"If you solved this content problem, what would change in your acquisition channels?

Imagine 6 months from now: your account grows 2,000 followers weekly,
all highly qualified leads—what's that worth to your business?

You'd have nothing to manage yourself.
What could you do with that time instead? Strategy? New markets?"
```

**Completion criteria**: Customer shows visible emotion shift (urgency OR excitement)

**Hermes optimization**: Detect tone/pace changes in transcription. Score emotional intensity. If muted, recommend which pulling angle is more effective for this customer.

---

#### 2.4 G4 - Guide Commitment (5 minutes)

**Objective**: Get customer to state their own commitment to change

**Four-Step Script**:

```
Step 1 - Summarize:
"So if I understand right: you want to do content but lack a reliable system,
 your account has stalled while competitors accelerate—is that fair?"

Step 2 - Expand distance:
"If you keep operating this way, in 6 months will this problem fix itself?"

Step 3 - Get commitment (CRITICAL):
"So do you want to find a way to fix this completely, or keep going as-is?"

→ This sentence determines deal probability:
  "Want to fix it now" = 75%+ close rate
  "Need to think about it" = 25% close rate

Step 4 - Confirm timing:
"If we had a solution, when would you want to start?"
```

**Completion criteria**: Customer explicitly states "I want to solve this now" or gives specific timeline

**Hermes optimization**: Auto-detect commitment signals. Flag if customer says "I'll think about it" (low probability). Recommend next steps based on answer quality.

---

#### 2.5 G5 - Value Display (4 minutes)

**Objective**: Show the shortest path to the vision; trigger active inquiry

**Core principle**: Results only, no process talk. Cases, not features.

**Three-Lever Framework**:

```
① Establish expert positioning:
"We focus only on [vertical], we don't generalize.
 So our methods align perfectly with your situation."

② Proof via case study:
"We worked with a [vertical] similar to you: went from 0 to 12K followers in 3 months,
 generated 23 qualified inquiries."

③ Different delivery model:
"You don't manage content production. Every Friday you get next week's content,
 spend 10 minutes approving it. That's it.
 We handle strategy, production, scheduling, analytics."
```

**Key**: Don't say "We have AI, automation…" → Customer doesn't care.
**Do say**: "You don't touch it, 10 minutes per week, we own everything else."

**Completion criteria**: Customer asks "How do you do that?" or inquires about the process

**Hermes optimization**: Query case library for "most relevant same-industry example." Auto-generate "key 3 numbers that would most impress this customer."

---

#### 2.6 G6 - Close (4 minutes)

**Objective**: Price, handle objections, collect deposit

**Pricing talk track**:
```
"Here's what we offer:

[Plan Name], [Price]/month, includes [core deliverables].

[STOP. Say nothing. Wait for them to respond.]"

← Silence is gold. Most reps over-talk here and kill the close.
```

**Objection handling matrix** (Sandler reversal method):

| If they say… | Reverse with… |
|---------|----------|
| **"Too expensive"** | "Expensive compared to what—compared to a full-time employee, or compared to doing nothing?" |
| **"Let employees try first"** | "I support that. Let's set a 30-day checkpoint. We look at: how many posts, follower growth, employee hours invested. You decide. Fair?" |
| **"Let me think about it"** | "Sure. What specifically are you considering—price, results potential, or internal discussions?" |
| **"Can you guarantee results?"** | "I won't promise numbers, but I promise execution: consistent weekly posting, monthly health report, if we see 3 weeks of decline I'll propose strategy changes. Sound good?" |

**Close push statements** (small-step closing per Sandler):
```
"Here's what we'll do: you put down a deposit,
 we launch diagnostic phase, collect your data.
 If it's not right, full refund. OK?"

Or

"You said you want to solve this now—
 let's start today. Sign this agreement, make the deposit,
 we kick off diagnostics next week."
```

**Completion criteria**: Deposit received or LOI signed

**OpenClaw tracking**: Record deal amount, contract date, start date in customer file. Mark "Entered G7 nurture system."

### Phase 3: Follow-Up Promotion (G7 - The 30-Day Rhythm)

#### 3.1 Days 1-7: Trust Loop

| Day | Action | Content | Purpose |
|-----|--------|---------|---------|
| **D1** | Thank + Diagnosis | Send diagnostic report + thank you message | Give value first, don't push; build reciprocity |
| **D3** | Industry insight | "Your industry had [trend/competitor move] this week. I saw the structure, matches your positioning…" | Show you're actively tracking their space; social proof |
| **D5** | Case proof | "Our client's content got approved this week. Numbers look strong. Want to see?" | Social proof: "everyone is doing this" |
| **D7** | Light close | "About a week in. Any new thoughts? I can hold your spot a bit longer." | Check progress; create scarcity (limited slots) |

**D1 Email Template**:
```
Subject: [Company Name] Content Operations Diagnostic Report

[Name],

Thank you for today. Here's what we found:

What's working:
✓ Account foundation: [follower count, cadence, quality]
What's not:
✗ Main bottleneck: [their pain points]
Competitive gap:
△ You vs. competitor: [specific comparison]

Attached: Competitor analysis (PDF)

My recommendation: [one-liner]

Reach out anytime.
```

**D3 Example**:
```
One more thing—I saw this case this week:
"[Brand] hit 10x growth in 3 months with [method]"

Their content structure and positioning actually mirrors yours pretty closely.

Want me to send you the breakdown?
There are a few details that might spark ideas for your operation.
```

**D5 Example**:
```
Quick win:

One of our recent clients (also in [category]) launched their content this week.
Initial results:

- 800+ likes in 3 days
- 120 comments
- 12 qualified inquiries from store visitors

Same methodology we discussed.
Interested in seeing full metrics?
```

**D7 Example**:
```
Just checking in—

Did the diagnostic report help?
Any internal discussions about next steps?

Oh, I should mention: I have a slot available through end of week.
Next week it moves to the waitlist.

When do you think you'll have a decision?
```

#### 3.2 Days 14-30: Value + Final Push

| Day | Action | Content |
|-----|--------|---------|
| **D14** | Second value round | "Your industry has [holiday/event]. I drafted content strategy. Does this fit your approach?" |
| **D21** | Final close | "[Name], you mentioned waiting a bit. Any conclusion now? I'm holding your spot." |
| **D30** | Archive decision | "If no signal: archive for long-term nurture. Monthly industry report to keep warmth." |

**In OpenClaw**: Automate D1-D7 reminders. Content library auto-populates industry insights. Email auto-sends (but recommend human review for personalization). Track open rates, reply status.

## FAQ & Problem-Solving

### Q: Customer says "We need to pilot this first"

**Answer** (Sandler reversal):

```
"I completely support that. Let me suggest: 30-day checkpoint.
We review three metrics:
1. How many posts we delivered (quality)
2. Follower growth
3. Hours your team spent managing

You decide then if it's worth continuing. Deal?"

Customer agrees.

"Perfect. Let's sign the pilot agreement today, put down a deposit.
We start next week. OK?"
```

**Why it works**: Converts "casual trial" into "structured test" with clear success criteria.

### Q: Customer asks price too early

**Response**:

```
Don't mention price in G1-G2.

Mention by G5 latest (after they see value).

When asked early:
"Price depends on scope. Let me understand what you need first,
then I can give you an accurate number. Sound good?"
```

### Q: No response by D7

**Standard handling**:

```
If they said "I'll think about it" on D7:
"Let's reconnect in a month? I'll have fresh case studies by then."
→ Schedule D30

If complete silence:
"Sending you our monthly industry report—just FYI, not a sales thing."
→ Archive as "long-term nurture"
→ Low-frequency contact (monthly)
```

### Q: How to spot a "real" vs. "fake" prospect?

**Red flags for fake prospects**:
- Can't pass G4 (won't commit to wanting change)
- Asks only "What features do you have?" (no self-reflection)
- Can never meet with decision-maker
- Repeatedly cancels

**Green flags for real prospects**:
- Said "I want to solve this now" in G4
- Asks process/timeline details (not just features)
- Decision-maker showed up
- Asks about next steps after G5

## Key Takeaway

Clover A-sales works because it respects the customer's decision-making process:
- Day 1: "Here's your urgent problem"
- Days 2-7: "We understand you + we can solve this"
- Days 8-30: "The window for this solution is limited, decide now"

When customers see the structure, the urgency, and the expertise—they buy.
